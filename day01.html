The temptation on Day 1 is to do too much. Provision all eight VMs today. Set up Postgres. Pull the first dataset. Resist all of it. Day 1 has a small, precise scope: turn the host into a hypervisor; turn three laptops into a team; verify the work that comes after has a place to land. Days 2 through 5 build on top of this; Days 6 through 20 build on top of those. If today is over-extended we will find ourselves on Day 8 still re-doing Day 1 work because nobody documented why br-poc0 uses the 10.20 range, or why we picked ZFS over LVM. The payoff for a disciplined Day 1 is not on Day 1 — it is two weeks from now when an unrelated investigation lands cleanly because the foundation under it is named.

One specific anti-goal worth naming: we do not provision the production VMs today. The seven service VMs (vm-core-api, vm-tox-inference, vm-embed-search, vm-rag-llm, vm-data-store, vm-worker-pool, vm-observability) all wait for Day 2. Today only ships the bastion. The reason is sequencing: the production VMs need br-poc0 on 10.20.0.0/24 and dnsmasq for vm-*.poc.local resolution, neither of which exists today — both arrive Day 2. Doing today what should be done tomorrow is how POCs slip on Day 6 because Day 1's work has to be redone with the right network configuration.

Each engineer named one "today's risk" in standup. The cross-cutting block named two more. Five total. Below: what each risk actually did, and how it was resolved or carried forward.

Risks have four dispositions in this register. Retired means the risk no longer exists — the structural condition that made it a risk is gone. Mitigated means the risk is still possible in principle but the mitigation worked today; we keep watching but it is not actively threatening. Fired means the risk became real; we consumed time on it; we record the impact. Live means it is carrying forward into a future day with active monitoring.
