Three endpoints compose into one explanation.

Today's /v1/evidence is the third leg of a tripod. By end of Day 9, when a toxicologist asks "explain why this compound predicts NR-AhR-negative", the platform composes:

Today's evidence index is what gives the Day-9 LLM something grounded to cite. Without it the LLM would hallucinate citations; with it, every claim in the natural-language explanation traces back to a PMID + MeSH-tagged sentence with a publication year. The composition is the regulator-facing surface area: every prediction comes with structural neighbors and published evidence, not just a probability.

Two halves of the pipeline. Build-time runs once today: scrape audit → 16-chunk chord → sentence-chunk → BGE embed → ChromaDB upsert. V1 plan rebuilds weekly via cron with delta-only updates for new abstracts; POC accepts a snapshot. Query-time runs per /v1/evidence request: BGE-embed the query (~18 ms), ChromaDB top-k query (~14 ms in a 4.3M-vector index), enrich + serialize. End-to-end p95 budget 44 ms; alert at 50 ms gives 12% headroom.

Worth noting what is not in this pipeline. No re-ranking — BGE-small is the only model in the stack; V1 may layer a cross-encoder re-rank step on the top-50 BGE results before returning top-10. No section-level segmentation — we index abstract sentences only, not full-text sections (intro/methods/results/discussion). No deduplication — some abstracts are near-duplicates (review articles citing each other); V1 adds sim-hash dedup. No author-disambiguation, no funder-tagging, no retraction-checking — all V1 surface area. Today is the simple shape: one corpus, one embedder, one collection, one cosine query.
